Signal Rail is a plain-text document governance system for personal and AI-assisted projects. It gives every piece of project material a destination — a specific file with a specific role — so nothing gets buried, promoted too early, or confused with something it is not. Think of it as a set of rails that keep your project readable while it grows, branches, changes shape, and comes back after interruption.

## How Signal Rail works

Signal Rail prevents the most common failure in project notes: everything living in the same undifferentiated space. A note becomes a decision. A possibility becomes a constraint. A current blocker gets buried under old context.

Signal Rail fixes this by asking one practical question before any material is written:

> **What kind of material is this, and where should it live?**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Bootstrap an instance">
    Run `init_signal_rail.bat` (Windows) or copy the canonical files manually into the folder you want to govern. The instance is a copy of Signal Rail living beside or inside your real project.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open with an AI agent">
    Ask your AI to read `00_runtime_entry.txt`, then `06_ai_to_ai.txt`. This activates the lateral kernel and establishes the operating frame before any action is taken.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Close the working frame">
    The agent closes the minimum frame: host project, working object, active mode, source scope, and authority. No writing happens until the frame is closed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Route material to the right rail">
    Current live work goes to `03_master_working.txt`. Decisions already won go to `04_decision_log.txt`. Strong-but-unresolved ideas go to `05_latent_ideas.txt`. Everything has a home.
  </Step>
</Steps>
